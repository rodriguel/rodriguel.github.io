Webpage of R.L.

I am Maître de Conférences (~ associate professor) at Université Paris 1 Panthéon-Sorbonne, at the SAMM lab since September ’25. Before that, I was a postdoctoral fellow at École des ponts et chaussées working with Virginie Ehrlacher, Tony Lelièvre, and Julien Reygner. I completed my PhD at Université Paris-Dauphine under the supervision of Mathieu Lewin.

You can contact me at firstname.name@univ-paris1.fr.

Research interests

  • Theory and numerics of optimal transport, notably its multimarginal extension as it appears in density functional theory in quantum chemistry; q
  • Study of classical (and continuous) statistical physics systems such as Coulomb and Riesz gases, with a particular focus on the existence of the thermodynamic limit and phase transitions.
  • Model order reduction techniques for high-dimensional stochastic differential equations and Wasserstein gradient flows on manifolds.
