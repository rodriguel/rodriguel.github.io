I am Maître de Conférences (~ associate professor) at Université Paris 1 Panthéon-Sorbonne, at the SAMM lab since September ’25. Before that, I was a postdoctoral fellow at École des ponts et chaussées. Even before that, I completed my PhD at Université Paris-Dauphine under the supervision of Mathieu Lewin.

You can contact me at firstname.name@univ-paris1.fr.

News

  • Luca Nenna has been awarded a PIQ (Programme Inria Quadrant) for our project PiQuantO (Problems in Quantum Optimal Transport). We will explore quantum optimal transport(s) in the context of density functional theory.

Research interests

  • Theory and numerics of optimal transport, notably its multimarginal extension as it appears in density functional theory in quantum chemistry;
  • Study of classical (and continuous) statistical physics systems such as Coulomb and Riesz gases, with a particular focus on the existence of the thermodynamic limit and phase transitions;
  • Model order reduction techniques for high-dimensional stochastic differential equations and Wasserstein gradient flows on manifolds.
